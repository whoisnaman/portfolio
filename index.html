Designing and Commercialising a Dual-Finger Wearable Phone Support Accessory

1. Executive Summary

Role: Technical Product Manager & Designer

The Challenge: Designing an ergonomic, structurally sound mobile accessory while navigating overseas manufacturing constraints.

The Outcome: Successfully prototyped, manufactured, and launched a bespoke hardware product with an international supply chain.

2. Problem Identification & User Need

Standard single-finger phone grips often cause lateral strain during extended use. The market required a solution that distributed the device's weight more evenly. By engineering a dual-finger support architecture, we significantly improved ergonomics and structural stability for the user.

3. Technical Specifications & Prototyping

Material Selection: Smooth ABS plastic was selected for its high impact resistance and lightweight properties.

Surface Finish: A critical UX requirement was specifying a subtle semi-gloss finish. We explicitly rejected high-gloss and mirror-like finishes to prevent fingerprint accumulation and ensure a reliable, tactile grip.

Iteration: The physical lifecycle included extensive CAD drafting and rapid prototyping iterations before finalising the injection moulding tooling.

4. Go-To-Market (GTM) & Commercialisation

To drive initial user acquisition, I directed and designed technical product demo videos optimised for Instagram Reels, effectively communicating the mechanical benefits to a digital audience.
